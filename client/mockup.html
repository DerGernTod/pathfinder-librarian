I'm a GM. My party just captured a mitflit king and wants to sell them at the market of a nearby settlement. How should I handle this?

🤖

Selling a living, unique creature like a Mitflit King isn't a standard item transaction, so standard "Sell Items" rules don't perfectly apply. Here is the best way to adjudicate this using PF2e mechanics:

1. Earn Income (Diplomacy)

Treat this as finding a buyer for exotic goods. Have the party face make an Earn Income check using Diplomacy. The creature is Level 4, so the DC should be 19. The payout should be equivalent to a Level 4 task (approx. 5 gp per day spent arranging the sale).

2. Setting the Scene

Don't just let them click "sell". Make it an event. The settlement's guard might demand a "Monster Handling Fee" (1d4 x 10 gp). The blacksmith might want it for a sick gladiatorial pit, while the town guard wants it executed.

The blacksmith decided to buy the mitflit king. How could this affect the settlement's and the blacksmith's standing with the party? What else could happen to the mitflit king?

🤖

Here are a few narrative and mechanical hooks for this decision:

  • Reputation Shift (Faction): If the town is lawful good, selling a sapient creature to a blacksmith for gladiatorial games might lower the party's standing with the Town Guard, but raise it with the local Thieves' Guild or Underworld.
  • The Blacksmith's Debt: The blacksmith might not have liquid cash. He could offer a custom-crafted level 4 magical weapon (like a +1 Striking Warhammer) at a deep discount instead of gold.
  • The Mitflit's Retinue: A Mitflit King wouldn't be alone. The party might soon find their base of operations besieged by angry Mitflits trying to rescue their liege.

PF2e RAG can make mistakes. Verify critical mechanics with the PRD.
